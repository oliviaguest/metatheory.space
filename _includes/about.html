Hello! üçÉ metatheory.space is a monthly (every four weeks) reading circle, where we discuss in structured ways written works (e.g. preprints, journal articles, book chapters, books): a) written within or we deem them relevant to cognitive science; and b) from a metatheoretical perspective. That is to say, a perspective that takes into account relationships between theories, adjudications over theories, and any other considerations relating to theories, computational tractability, Ryle's regress, virtuous and vicious properties, and so on.

The works we discuss need not be theoretical themselves nor straightforwardly cognitive scientific. For example, empirical work can be proposed and then the frame would be to untangle the theoretical commitments implicit and explicit within such writing. If theoretical or computational modelling work is proposed, the same would apply anyway, to tease apart what the theory's commitments are and relate them to broader field level questions or desires, to critical treatments of their formalism and purpose, and so on. In the case of philosophical, or otherwise more obviously metatheoretical from the get go, work, we can also apply such frames to theories or methodology we're familiar with, for example.

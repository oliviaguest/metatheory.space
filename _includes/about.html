Hello! üçÉ metatheory.space is a monthly (every four weeks) reading circle, where we discuss in structured ways written works (e.g. preprints, journal articles, book chapters, books): a) written within or we deem them relevant to cognitive science; and b) from a metatheoretical perspective. That is to say, a perspective that takes into account relationships between theories, adjudications over theories, and any other considerations relating to theories, computational tractability, Ryle's regress, virtuous and vicious properties, whether the authors are naive in their computationalism, and so on {% cite guestmartin2025 %}.

The works we discuss need not be theoretical themselves nor straightforwardly cognitive scientific. For example, empirical (e.g. experimental psychology or neuroscience) or engineering (e.g. machine learning or artificial intellignece) work can be proposed and then the frame would be to untangle the theoretical commitments implicit and explicit within such writing. If theoretical or computational modelling work is proposed, the same would apply anyway: to tease apart what the theory's commitments are and relate them to broader field level questions or desires, to critical treatments of their formalism and purpose, and so on. In the case of philosophical, or otherwise more obviously metatheoretical from the get go, work, we can also apply frames within the target work to theories or methodology we are already familiar with, for example.

Analysis technique suggestions comprise many methods, from theoretical complexity analysis at the computational-level {% cite vanrooij2008 vanrooij2024 %}, to metatheoretical calculus analysis at the pre- and post-formal levels {% cite guestmartin2023 guestmartin2024 %}, to more zoomed-in analysies like separating vehicle versus content in computationalist theorising {% cite egan2020 %}. Furthermore, welcome are gender, coloniality, race, and other sociocultural consequences in the critique of theories, frameworks and general ways of doing and disseminating science {% cite guest2024 %}.

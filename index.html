metatheory.space

Hello! 🍃 metatheory.space is a group that works at the foundations of cognitive science. Exploring questions such as:

  • How do we build theory in cognitive science?
  • What does taking computationalism seriously imply?
  • How do we use formal models to reason about cognition?

about

We are interested in the intersections of cognitive science, especially psychological and computational neurocognitive theorising, and critical artificial intelligence with a focus on metatheoretical considerations at play in this vast interdisciplinary landscape.

values

We try our best to perform work that is radical and critical — and we especially welcome gender, coloniality, race, and other sociocultural consequences in the discussion of theories, of technologies, and of the general ways of performing and disseminating science (Guest, 2024).

Relatedly, we also value making space for work that not only respects these perspectives, but also is about researchers’ interests in and of themselves. There is something very radical in making space for voices that are not typically heard.

metatheory

Meta (from Greek, μετά) means something before or above another; and theory (also originally from Greek, θεωρία) is, for our intents and purposes, a scientific product with certain properties. Putting the two words together gives us “metatheory”, which hopefully readily implies ideas like: research about theories, thinking about theories, or theorising about theories, and so on.

Therefore, metatheory in the cognitive science context is a perspective that takes into account relationships between theories, adjudications over theories, and any other considerations relating to theories. Below are some core readings on our perspective:

    metamethodology

    Metamethodology and metatheory are related, with the former having more of an emphasis on understanding how computational cognitive science is done in the general case, i.e. involving modelling and theory. So while metatheory has an emphasis on how we reason about, over, and with theories — metamethodology involves describing how computational cognitive science is or could be practiced, how the mediation from models (e.g. formalisms, code) between theory and data is or can be carried out.

    The paper below (Guest et al., 2025) certainly falls under both meta-theory and -methodology, but take note that "reverse engineering" is a proposal about the nature of the link between models and phenomena, cognitive capacities, and data. Thus, it embodies a suggestion for how scientists should perform their science, not just reason over it.

    1. Guest, O., Scharfenberg, N., & van Rooij, I. (2025). Modern Alchemy: Neurocognitive Reverse Engineering. https://philsci-archive.pitt.edu/25289
